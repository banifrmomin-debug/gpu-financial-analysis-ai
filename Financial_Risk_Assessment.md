## Supply chain risk
Supply Chain Risk
NVIDIA relies on a fabless manufacturing strategy, depending heavily on third-party partners like TSMC for wafer fabrication and Amkor or SPIL for assembly and testing
Disruptions in the Pacific Rim or a lack of domestic U.S. manufacturing capacity could impair its ability to meet demand
## China restrictions
Export controls imposed by the U.S. Government (USG) have significantly impacted NVIDIA’s ability to sell high-performance AI chips to China
.
H20 Impact: In the first quarter of fiscal year 2026, NVIDIA incurred a $4.5 billion charge for excess inventory and purchase obligations related to H20 products after new licensing requirements diminished demand
.
Tier 2 Licensing: The USG's "AI Diffusion" rules require worldwide licensing for high-performance products achievement certain thresholds, effectively risking foreclosure from the China data center market
## AI competition
Competition is intensifying as rivals strengthen their portfolios and customers begin vertical integration
.
Direct Rivals: AMD is aggressively scaling its Instinct MI300X accelerators to capture AI market share
.
In-house Silicon: Large cloud service providers (CSPs) like Amazon, Alphabet, and Microsoft are developing internal hardware to replace third-party GPUs
.
Open Source AI: If developers prioritize open-source models optimized for competing platforms, it could weaken the CUDA ecosystem's influence
