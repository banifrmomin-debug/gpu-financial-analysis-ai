## AI Market Expansion

.
From Training to Reasoning: While AI growth was initially driven by model training, it is now shifting toward real-time reasoning (inference), which has become the dominant workload
.

## Data Center Revenue
The Data Center platform is NVIDIA's primary growth engine, representing the "new unit of computing" where thousands of nodes are interconnected to function as a single giant computer
.
Fiscal Year 2025 Performance: Data Center revenue reached $115.2 billion, a 142% increase year-over-year
.
Segment Breakdown:
Compute: Revenue grew 162%, fueled by massive demand for the Hopper architecture for large language models and recommendation engines
.
Networking: Revenue grew 51% to $13.0 billion, driven by the strong uptake of the Spectrum-X end-to-end Ethernet platform and InfiniBand
.
First Quarter Fiscal 2026: Data Center revenue was $39.1 billion, up 73% from a year ago
. This was driven by the continued ramp of Blackwell systems, which reached all customer categories, including large cloud service providers and consumer internet companies
.
Export Control Impacts: Despite overall growth, revenue in China has been significantly impacted by U.S. government licensing requirements
. In the first quarter of fiscal 2026, NVIDIA incurred a $4.5 billion charge for excess inventory and purchase obligations related to H20 products after new export licensing rules were imposed
.

NVIDIA maintains a strong engineering culture, with more than half of its engineers dedicated to software development.
Total Investment: The company has invested over  $12.9 billion, a 49% increase from fiscal year 2024
This surge was driven by employee growth, compensation increases, and the heavy engineering development costs required for the Blackwell architecture
Quarterly Growth: R&D spending reached $4.0 billion in the first quarter of fiscal year 2026, up 47% year-over-year
